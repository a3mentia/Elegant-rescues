Why one studio

Most objects aren't made of just one thing. Neither is the training behind them.

  • Metals & silverware
  • Ceramics & porcelain
  • Works on paper
  • Antique furniture & wood
  • Heritage buildings
  • Kintsugi-style repair

A chair with bone inlay. A frame that's part timber, part gilt. A building of stone and wood. When an object combines materials it usually gets passed between specialists - or only half-treated. Adri Loubser trained across all of them, so your piece is handled as a whole, by one person.

As far as we know, no other studio in South Africa works across all of these - and very few in Europe do.

What we restore

Six areas of work.

Each one begins the same way - an honest assessment of what your object needs, and a few options for how far to take it.

01

Paper conservation

Works on paper, documents and photographs - foxing, water and mould damage, tears and creasing, and the acidic marks left by an old mount or frame.

Explore → 02

Ceramic restoration

Ceramics, porcelain and glass - from a clean break to a shattered piece, unstable joins, the removal of old adhesives and the reversal of failing repairs.

Explore → 03

Furniture & wood

Repair, stabilisation and the recreation of missing detail - lost colour and patina, rot, cracks and flaking, woodworm, and water or smoke damage.

Explore → 04

Heritage buildings

Survey and sampling reports, moisture and mould diagnosis, and mortar analysis for older structures - and the rising-damp problems they bring.

Explore → 05

Metals conservation

Metals corrode and rust with time. Do you keep the patina or polish it? Accidental dents, breakages and staining are problems that I can assist with.

Explore → 06 · Signature

Kintsugi-style repairs

Repair made visible. Breaks rejoined and traced in gold, so an object's history becomes part of how it looks - rather than something to hide.

Adri Loubser · Conservator-restorer

The conservator

Two disciplines, one pair of hands.

Adri Loubser brings conservation training grounded in research together with years of structured project management - and applies both to every commission.

Her postgraduate work covered the treatment and structural stabilisation of ceramics, paper, metals and historic masonry, with advanced study in materials science, building pathology and preventive care. Her dissertation tackled one of the most stubborn problems in old buildings - rising damp.

Before conservation she spent 14+ years as a digital project manager, the last seven freelance. That's why work here is planned, documented and predictable - not a black box you hand your object into and hope.

You're always shown options and an honest view of what's worth doing. Every piece gets archival, museum-grade materials and the same attention - whether it's a museum-worthy antique or the chipped cup your grandmother loved.
